Digitization, digitalization, digitalization, or digitization is a general term for converting something into a digital format.

AERO3D company’s main activity profile is the digitization of the cultural heritage of Ukraine for its preservation: digitization of architectural and historical monuments, buildings, monuments, monuments, exhibits, creation of 3D Google tours and Matterport tours, individual 3D tours.

The range of possibilities of digitization in culture

We create 3D models of these objects using two technologies: photogrammetry and laser scanning. It is possible to use only photogrammetry – then we get a high-quality picture, but not clear dimensions. Or just a laser scan of the architecture – in this case, the dimensions and shapes will be perfectly preserved, but without a color picture, for example, a drawing. Therefore, we suggest combining these technologies to achieve an ideal result.

Digitalization can also be considered a created 3D tour of a museum or a 3D tour of an exhibition, an audio accompaniment of a 3D tour, a created digital exhibition of paintings, or an archive of museum exhibits on a modern and reliable platform.

About digitization in culture

Digitalization has become an important topic for the cultural sphere in recent years. This means the transition from traditional analog formats to digital formats for storing, processing, and transmitting the information. This process opens up new opportunities for the preservation, access, and dissemination of cultural resources, as well as for attracting new audiences to cultural heritage.

Examples of digitization in culture and beyond

One of the most obvious examples of digitization in culture is the transition to electronic books, audiobooks, and other electronic publication formats. This allows you to quickly and easily access books and other literature from anywhere with Internet access. In addition, digitization allows books and other resources to be stored electronically, reducing the risk of loss or damage.

Digitization also allows museum and archival collections to create digital copies of their resources, allowing a wider public to access cultural assets. In addition, it allows cultural resources to be preserved digitally, reducing the risk of their loss or damage.

Digitalization also enables the development of new forms of art and culture. For example, virtual reality and other technologies allow the creation of new impressions and experiences for viewers and listeners. In addition, digital tools allow artists and creators to create more complex and interactive works using various digital technologies. For example, virtual art exhibitions, interactive games based on cultural and historical events, multimedia performances and more.

Digitalization also allows cultural institutions and organizations to open up to new formats of audience engagement and the development of cooperation. For example, this can be the creation of online courses and training programs, the use of social networks, and other online tools to communicate with the audience and promote interaction.

Challenges of digitization

However, digitization also introduces new challenges and problems, in particular with regard to the preservation and protection of intellectual property, the spread of unauthorized copying and piracy, and the increasing inequality of access to cultural resources.

Therefore, digitalization is becoming more and more important for the cultural sphere, but it is necessary to carefully monitor its development and to solve the new challenges and problems that it introduces.

Russian aggression in the east of Ukraine led to the destruction and destruction of numerous cultural and historical monuments of Ukraine.

In particular, in the period from 2014 to 2022, many architectural monuments, including churches, castles and palaces, were destroyed. For example, the Intercession Church in the city of Sloviansk, located in the Donetsk region, was completely destroyed. Monuments of military architecture were also destroyed, in particular the fortress in the city of Luhansk.

At the same time, many museum collections, archaeological finds and other artifacts were stolen, destroyed or transported to Russia. For example, more than 800 exhibits, including rare books and paintings, were stolen from the open-air museum “Stary Krym” in Feodosia.

At the same time, the Russian occupation authorities introduce their own ideological and cultural standards, which lead to the destruction of Ukrainian culture and identity. For example, in the city of Sevastopol, which was occupied by Russian troops, an architectural monument was destroyed – the colonial villa “Krasny Pyatachok”, which was recognized as one of the symbols of the city.

In addition, in February 2021, it was reported that the Russian authorities destroyed more than 300 archives in Crimea. These archives stored historical documents, in particular, about the life of the Crimean Tatars and their repression during the Stalin era.

Also, as a result of the war in Donbas, many cultural objects were damaged or destroyed. For example, in the city of Avdiivka, the historical church of St. Nicholas and the Chekhov library were destroyed. In the city of Mariupol, a large collection of books and paintings was stored in the local art museum, but some of the exhibits were destroyed during hostilities.

In addition, Ukrainian cultural figures who do not support Russian aggression in Ukraine have become the object of persecution and repression. In particular, Ukrainian directors Oleg Sentsov and Serhiy Loznytsia were detained and convicted in Russia.

In general, the war in Ukraine has a serious impact on the cultural life of the country, destroying not only material objects, but also causing the loss of historical memory and cultural heritage.

The full scale of the Russian invasion of Ukraine resulted in the damage or destruction of more than 1,500 objects of cultural heritage and cultural infrastructure.

The Minister of Culture and Information Policy, Oleksandr Tkachenko, spoke about this at the international forum on cultural heritage security “War in Ukraine: Battle for Culture”. According to information from the regions, hundreds of cultural heritage sites, dozens of museums, libraries and archives have been damaged or destroyed, and the number is increasing daily as the frontline keeps shifting and the Russians resort to rocket attacks and artillery fire. According to Tkachenko, some objects of cultural heritage were specially selected for destruction.

As of March 18, 2023, the website culturecrimes.mkip.gov.ua states “The number of damaged and destroyed objects of cultural heritage and cultural institutions of Ukraine recorded in the database is 553.”

]]> https://aero3d.ua/en/rosiisko-ukrainska-viina-kulturna-spadshchyna-ukrainy/feed/ 0 What is laser scanning? https://aero3d.ua/en/shcho-take-fotogrametriia/ https://aero3d.ua/en/shcho-take-fotogrametriia/#respond Mon, 06 Mar 2023 20:10:30 +0000 https://aero3d.ua/?p=4312 Laser scanning is a technology that uses a laser beam to create an accurate three-dimensional model of an object or surface. This technology has found widespread use in various fields, including architecture, engineering, construction, cultural heritage, and industry.

The laser scanning process consists of several stages. First, preliminary preparation is carried out, which includes setting up the scanner and connecting it to a computer. The scanner then uses a laser beam to read the surface of the object and collects data about its shape, texture, and colors.

After data collection, processing is carried out, which includes editing and aligning the data to create an accurate three-dimensional model of the object. This can be done using special software that allows for the creation of large and complex models.

The obtained model can be used to measure various parameters of the object, from area and volume to distance between different points. Laser scanning can also be used to create high-quality topographic maps and models of the terrain.

One of the most interesting applications of laser scanning is the preservation of cultural heritage. Many historical and cultural buildings have been destroyed or damaged by time, wars, and natural disasters. Thanks to laser scanning, it is possible to restore and virtualize these structures.

Igor Sikorsky is one of the greatest and most famous aircraft designers in the world. International Airport “Kyiv” named after I. Sikorsky and Kyiv Polytechnic Institute named after Ihor Sikorsky are named after him in Kyiv.

Despite the fact that Kyiv houses the house where the world aviation genius was born, Ukraine does not have a Museum named after him. Consequently, Ukraine is losing a huge potential for popularizing the country of talents. The creation of the SIKOR SKY virtual MUSEUM solves this problem to a large extent by highlighting, demonstrating and popularizing the history of Igor Sikorsky.

In 2019, the head of our company AERO3D – Serhii Prysiazhnyi and Olga Barkova – the head of the platform DC-Visu, the NGO “GraDeSvit” started work on the creation of a virtual 3D museum of Igor Sikorsky – a world aviation genius who was born and took the first steps to recognition in Kyiv.

Thanks to this project, the problem of digital 3D preservation of a historic building, which is in a state of emergency, is solved, the creation of its identical digital 3D model, the possibility of its further use for carrying out repair and restoration works of the building.*

Multimedia-immersive technology provides deep immersion in the virtual museum exhibition as a creative product, which contributes to obtaining one’s own experience of experiencing the era in general and the biography of Igor Sikorsky in particular – in the historical, cultural and technical aspects of the inventions of Ukrainian pioneers of world aviation.

The painstaking work of our team on the implementation of the idea lasted half a year, and in 2020 we presented the online museum.

To implement the project, laser scanning technologies with photogrammetry were used, as a safe, non-contact technology of three-dimensional imaging of an object to create its digital image using an unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV, drone). We processed the received photos in special software and as a result received a 3D model</a > a real existing house.

The next step was the creation of a virtual reconstruction of the first floor (with interiors, furniture and decoration in accordance with the modern era of late XIX-early XX centuries), as well as a virtual hub space of 2-3 floors.

Unfortunately, the estate in which the famous aircraft designer once lived is now in a state of disrepair, and no photographs of its former appearance have been preserved. Therefore, in order to reproduce in the most accurate form all the subtleties of the interior, it was necessary to rely on the era, wealth, needs and tastes of the Sikorsky family, to involve historical experts. With the help of 3D modeling, each household item in the house was created separately.

We have created a digital museum where you can walk through the corridors and rooms, examine the objects, get more detailed information about each object in the digital collection, visit additional exhibitions – music and painting.

Digitization of the aircraft designer itself was possible thanks to cooperation with the Museum of the Formation of the Nation – it is there that the silicone figure of I. Sikorsky is kept.

In addition to the virtual 3d tour museum, which allows anyone to walk around the house on a Google map, for the clever there is a quest-museum, which is downloaded on the computer as a game and has 52 interactive test tasks. Unreal Engine was used for gamification of interactive tours.

We hope that later on the basis of our work, the physical restoration of the house, as well as of our entire great country, will be carried out!

Imagining is good, and now we offer a short video to take a walk through the virtual museum. We are waiting for your questions, feedback in YouTube comments, because we are all Ukrainians and want to be better together!
